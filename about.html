About Me

I'm a data science apprentice working toward a BSc, expected to graduate in August 2027. Alongside my studies, I'm building expertise at the crossroads of land management, agriculture, and environmental data.

Over four years of apprenticeship I've developed hands-on skills across the full data stack — from wrangling messy real-world datasets to building automated pipelines and communicating insight to non-technical audiences. I'm now applying those skills to a set of domains I find genuinely compelling: food production, rural land use, and the natural environment.

This site is both a portfolio and a working notebook. The projects here are real analyses — not toy examples — and I publish my code, methodology, and findings openly. I'm interested in working with farmers, estate managers, conservation organisations, and anyone trying to make sense of their land data.

Areas of Focus

  • Agri-data consultancy — Precision agriculture, farm performance analytics, soil and yield data.
  • Landed estate advisory — Technology strategy, data infrastructure, and reporting for rural estates.
  • Natural capital — Ecosystem service valuation, biodiversity metrics, carbon accounting.
